Part 1: Identifying Usability Problems
Picking a Webpage
Picking a Webpage
I chose this website because I’m incredibly excited about the future of digital journalism. While the site is functional, and generally quite accessible, there are definitely areas of improvement in terms of information and visual hierarchy.
Finding Problems
Desktop
 LEARNABILITY: Opinions are the least viewed section, but it takes up a lot of space on the main page above the fold
LEARNABILITY: Two needs from readers – Most important and most recent, but only have most important
LEARNABILITY: The headings for each section are not clearly marked as drop-downs
EFFICIENCY: No indication of length of the article
EFFICIENCY: No dedicated pages for extended coverage of one topic, only broken down by section
MEMORABILITY: “…” at the end of summaries on the front page. Should use the sumdek provided by writers.

Mobile
 EFFICIENCY: “About, donate, etc” top bar should be on the bottom on mobile, takes up valuable screen space for information that users are not there to see
MEMORABILITY: More section at the bottom has no sumdek, images, or accompanying information
LEARNABILITY: No way to view dropdowns of subheadings on mobile

Accessibility
 The alt text on the images were simply the file names, which typically are just the names of the photographers and authors. This should be replaced by better descriptions of the images, and can be replaced by the image captions as default, which are included in the articles.
It said that several links were “empty” as they contained no text, but they have sufficient aria labels, indicating both their purpose and their visual.
There were several low contrast errors for the very top header and lowest footer, which could be easily fixed by increasing the contrast.
There are many adjacent links, where both the image and the headline click into the article. This could be replaced by a link that encompasses the entire article card.
